Part One

The Lay of the Land

This guide assumes you know your job. You run a corporate cash portfolio; you speak to money market desks, bank relationship managers, fund company representatives, and counterparty treasury teams daily. What it does not assume is that you have ever opened Claude Desktop's Cowork tab.

The goal is a working prospecting workflow: one that researches institutions, surfaces contacts, drafts outreach, and maintains a running contact log without you coordinating every step. No code is written, no technical configuration is required, and no platform terms are violated. Every tool referenced here is either a native Anthropic product or a verified connector from the February or May 2026 launches.

The Eight Layers

Claude Cowork is not a conversational interface. It is an autonomous agent inside the Claude Desktop app that takes multi-step action on your behalf: reading files, navigating your browser, writing documents, querying connected data services, and publishing outputs to GitHub Pages. The distinction from standard chat is structural: you describe an outcome and Claude executes the steps required to reach it, surfacing reasoning as it goes and asking for course corrections only when genuinely needed.

A fully configured setup for a cash investment SVP stacks eight layers. This guide covers setup and operation of all of them.

Part Two

Setup

Get the Right Plan and Download the App

Cowork requires a paid plan. Pro ($20/month) works but reaches usage limits faster on complex research tasks. Max ($100/month) is the practical choice for regular sessions. Enterprise covers team-wide shared plugins and admin controls.

Download Claude Desktop from claude.com/download. Available for macOS and Windows. Sign in with your Anthropic account and confirm you see three tabs: Chat, Cowork, Code.

Install the Financial Services Plugins

Go to Settings → Customize → Plugins. Install two plugins to start:

Financial Analysis, the core plugin. Sets up connector slots for FactSet, Morningstar, S&P Global, Moody's, LSEG, PitchBook, and others. Your starting point for all research tasks.

Sales handles contact research and CRM integration. Includes a /call-prep skill and integrations with Apollo, Clay, HubSpot, and Salesforce.

Claude walks you through setup via questions about your role. Answer accurately; your responses tune the skills to your context.

Connect Your Data Sources

Go to Settings → Customize → Connectors. Authorize the sources relevant to your workflow:

FactSet S&P Capital IQ LSEG Morningstar PitchBook Moody's MCP Apollo Clay Gmail Google Calendar Google Drive

Apollo is now a native Claude connector. Search, enrich, create contacts, and queue outreach sequences directly from Claude without switching applications.

FactSet / SPGI give Claude real-time access to AUM data, fund flows, institutional ownership, and executive contact data. Authorization requires an API key from your data provider's account administrator.

Clay is optional and suited to high-volume prospecting. It runs a data waterfall across dozens of sources to find verified emails and phone numbers ($149+/month).

Enable Claude in Chrome

In Connectors, find Claude in Chrome and toggle it on. Included at no extra cost. Claude prioritizes connectors first, falls back to the browser when needed, and always asks permission before accessing a new application.

This is how it reaches company IR pages, regulatory filings sites, event attendee lists, and conference speaker directories that aren't integrated via API.

Create a Cowork Project

In the Cowork tab, create a New Project. Name it "Finance Prospecting: Cash Investments." Projects are persistent, self-contained workspaces with their own memory, files, and instructions. Everything accumulates over time.

Add this instruction set in the project context field; paste it in and adjust the specifics to match your actual mandate:

→ Project Context Instructions

I am a Senior Vice President and Portfolio Manager in corporate cash investment management. When researching contacts, I am looking for: Treasurer and Assistant Treasurer roles at corporations with $500M+ cash balances, relationship managers at primary dealer banks (fixed income / cash management desks), fund company representatives covering institutional money market and ultra-short bond products, and custodian bank contacts (securities lending, collateral management). Prioritize contacts at firms where we have existing or potential business relationships. Output contact lists in tabular format with name, title, firm, LinkedIn URL, email (if available), and a one-line note on why this contact is relevant.

On Project Context

This instruction set fires on every task inside this project, eliminating the need to re-establish role context at the start of each session. Cowork's project memory accumulates over time, recognizing patterns in your coverage universe and applying your preferred output formats without prompting.

Part Three

Prospecting Workflows

Four tasks you can assign to Cowork as completed deliverables. Describe the outcome, submit the task, and return to finished work.

WF — 01

Build a Targeted Contact List from Scratch

Manually cross-referencing Bloomberg, FactSet, and LinkedIn before a coverage expansion or product launch

In the Cowork tab inside your project, type or paste a task like this:

→ Sample Task Prompt

Build me a contact list of corporate treasury decision-makers at investment-grade US companies with publicly reported cash balances above $1 billion. Focus on Treasurer, VP Finance, and Director of Treasury roles. For each contact, I want: full name, title, company, estimated cash balance (from public filings), LinkedIn profile URL, and whether they appear in any recent conference speaker lists for AFP, TMA, or EuroFinance. Export the result to a spreadsheet in my Google Drive Prospecting folder.

Claude queries your connected financial data sources for companies meeting the cash balance threshold, uses Apollo to find confirmed contacts by title keyword, uses Claude in Chrome to cross-reference conference speaker directories, and compiles the results into a formatted Google Drive spreadsheet.

The task runs 10 to 30 minutes depending on list size and can proceed without your attention.

Realistic Output

50–200 verified contacts per run, with quality varying based on your data connector subscriptions. Apollo-sourced contacts typically run 80–90% email verification rates. FactSet / SPGI cash balance data is institutional grade.

WF — 02

Research a Prospect Before Outreach

20 minutes of tab-switching before a call — Bloomberg for firm profile, Google for the person, LinkedIn, then mentally assembling the brief
→ Sample Task Prompt

Prepare a pre-call brief for [Name], [Title] at [Company]. Include: their professional background and tenure, their firm's cash management profile (size, known banking relationships, any public commentary on investment policy), recent relevant news (M&A, credit rating changes, earnings notes on cash), any conference panels or published content they've been part of, and three tailored talking points given my focus on [your product or service area].

Claude pulls from connected data sources and browser navigation, returning a one-page brief ready two minutes before the call. Save these briefs to your Prospecting project folder in Drive. Cowork's project memory accumulates context on your coverage universe over time.

WF — 03

Draft and Queue Personalized Outreach

Manually writing individual emails for a coverage campaign, or using generic templates with low reply rates

Once you have a contact list from Workflow 1 or an existing CRM export:

→ Sample Task Prompt

I'm attaching a prospect list [paste or upload CSV]. For each contact, draft a personalized first-touch email from my perspective as an SVP in corporate cash investment management. Each email should: reference something specific about their firm or role (use connected data to find a relevant hook), lead with value rather than an ask, run 4–5 sentences, and end with a soft call to action for a 20-minute intro call. Save drafts in a Google Sheet with columns for contact name, firm, email address, and draft message body. Flag any contacts where you couldn't find a relevant hook; I'll handle those manually.

If the Outreach connector is enabled, Claude can add these contacts directly to an Outreach sequence, removing the export and re-import step entirely.

Tone Calibration

Review 10 drafts before approving the full batch on first use. If the register is off, add a correction to your project instructions: "Draft outreach in a formal but direct tone. Avoid 'I hope this finds you well.' Do not mention AI assistance." Cowork's project memory applies this to all subsequent sessions.

WF — 04

Morning Market Intelligence Digest (Recurring)

Manually curating a briefing from Bloomberg alerts, news services, and industry newsletters

Cowork supports scheduled recurring tasks. Configure this one to run on weekdays at 6:45 AM:

→ Recurring Task Prompt

Every weekday morning, compile a 1-page market intelligence digest for a corporate cash portfolio manager. Include: overnight Fed funds activity and money market rate moves, any notable CP/CD issuance or spread changes, earnings releases or credit events at names in my coverage universe [attach watchlist], any new regulatory guidance relevant to corporate cash investment, and one or two items from financial conference news or industry publications about corporate treasury trends. Format as a 5-minute read. Save to Google Drive and send a summary to my Gmail.

The digest runs while you sleep and is available on arrival at your desk.

Part Four

Scheduled Tasks

Scheduled tasks are one of the more operationally significant features in Cowork for a cash portfolio role. Rather than initiating each task manually, you configure it once with a recurrence pattern and a time, and Cowork executes it on schedule while the desktop app is running. The output is delivered to a location you specify: a Google Drive folder, a Gmail summary, a Sheet that updates in place.

The setup is in the Cowork tab under Tasks → Schedule. You write the task description exactly as you would for a one-time task, then set frequency (daily, weekly, on specific weekdays) and execution time. Cowork surfaces a confirmation before the first run and logs each subsequent execution in your task history.

For a cash investment SVP, three scheduling patterns are worth establishing at the outset. The first is the daily morning digest described in Workflow 4: overnight rate moves, CP/CD spread changes, and credit events in your watchlist, delivered before the open. The second is a weekly coverage refresh, run Friday afternoons, that re-queries Apollo and your connected financial data sources against your existing contact list to surface title changes, firm moves, and new names that meet your criteria. The third is a monthly counterparty intelligence pass: a deeper research task that scans public filings, ratings actions, and news for your active counterparties and writes a structured summary to Drive.

On Scheduling Logistics

Claude Desktop must be open and your computer awake for scheduled tasks to execute. If you close the app or your machine sleeps through the scheduled time, the task will not run for that cycle. For tasks where consistent execution matters (the morning digest, for example), a dedicated machine left running overnight or a wake schedule configured in your OS settings resolves this. Cowork does not currently support cloud-side scheduling for Cowork tasks; that execution model is available via Claude Managed Agents on the Claude Platform for teams that need guaranteed delivery.

On Prompt Precision for Scheduled Tasks

Scheduled task prompts should be more explicit than one-time task prompts on three points: the output location (exact Drive folder path or Sheet name), what to do when nothing new is found (write a brief "no material changes" entry rather than skipping the file entirely), and how to handle ambiguous matches (flag them for review rather than resolving them autonomously). A task that runs unattended at 6:45 AM has no opportunity for mid-run clarification, so the more precisely the prompt handles edge cases in advance, the more reliable the output.

Part Five

Important Boundaries

What Claude Will Not Do

Cowork blocks investment and trading platforms by default. Claude cannot execute trades, enter orders, or interact with your portfolio management system. These workflows operate at the relationship and intelligence layer only.

LinkedIn Automation

Claude in Chrome can navigate LinkedIn and read public profile information. Automating connection requests or messages at scale violates LinkedIn's Terms of Service. The compliant paths are: Apollo (native connector) for contact data discovery and email outreach, since Apollo sources its data through licensed means rather than real-time scraping; LinkupAPI as an MCP connector for programmatic LinkedIn search if your firm's legal team approves it; and Claude in Chrome for research assistance when you are actively reviewing a profile, not for autonomous bulk outreach. LinkedIn Sales Navigator's official partner integrations are the fully compliant path at volume.

Data Subscriptions Are Required

The finance connectors (FactSet, MSCI, S&P, LSEG, PitchBook, Moody's) are data pipes. Claude provides the analysis layer on top, but underlying subscriptions are required for access. Apollo alone is sufficient to run Workflows 1 through 3 at a basic level if institutional data subscriptions are not in place.

Cowork Is Not a Compliance Tool

Outputs Claude produces, including contact lists, pre-call briefs, and outreach drafts, are analyst work product staged for review. Run outreach campaigns through your normal compliance review process.

Sensitive Data Stays Local

Cowork runs in an isolated VM on your computer. Documents processed locally do not leave your machine for training. Queries to connected data providers (FactSet, Apollo, etc.) do leave your machine subject to those providers' data terms. Review your firm's data classification policies before connecting any institutional data source.

Appendix A

Agent Skills

Exact creation prompts for the seven skills a cash investment SVP should install in their Finance Prospecting project. Each skill is a persistent instruction set Claude applies automatically whenever the relevant context is detected.

Skills in Claude Cowork are reusable instruction packages attached to a project. When you create a skill, you are writing a system-level prompt that Claude loads before executing any task that matches the skill's trigger conditions. The result is consistent behavior without re-prompting: your preferred output format, your analytical framework, your terminology and tone, applied automatically across every session in that project.

To create a skill, go to the Cowork tab, open your Finance Prospecting project, and select Project Settings → Skills → Add Skill. Paste the prompt below into the skill body, give it the name shown, and save. Claude will apply it from the next task onward.

Skill 01

Treasury Contact Classifier

TriggerContact research, prospect lists, name lookups
Connectors UsedApollo, FactSet, Claude in Chrome
OutputStructured contact table

This skill ensures that every contact Claude surfaces is evaluated against a consistent tiering framework before being added to a prospect list. Without it, Claude will include any contact with a matching title regardless of strategic relevance. With it, Claude applies your coverage logic automatically.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When researching, evaluating, or classifying finance contacts, apply the following tiering framework before including any name in an output list. Tier 1 — Primary targets: Treasurer, Assistant Treasurer, VP Treasury, Director of Treasury, Head of Cash Management, Chief Investment Officer at corporations with cash balances above $500M. These contacts have direct authority over cash investment decisions and counterparty selection. Tier 2 — Influence contacts: CFO, VP Finance, Director of Finance at corporations with cash balances $200M–$500M; Relationship Managers and Fixed Income Sales at primary dealer banks covering corporate cash; Institutional Sales at money market and ultra-short bond fund companies covering corporate accounts. Tier 3 — Context contacts: Investor Relations officers, Treasury Analysts, Controllers. Include these only when no Tier 1 or Tier 2 contact is available at a target firm, and flag them clearly as secondary. For every contact output, include: full name, title, tier classification (1, 2, or 3), firm name, estimated AUM or cash balance where available, verified email (Apollo), LinkedIn URL, and a one-line relevance note. Sort all lists by tier, then alphabetically by firm within each tier. Flag any contact where title or firm data is unconfirmed with [UNVERIFIED] in the relevance note field.

Skill 02

Pre-Call Research Protocol

TriggerPre-call brief, call prep, meeting research
Connectors UsedFactSet, Claude in Chrome, Google Drive
OutputOne-page brief, Drive doc

This skill standardizes the structure and research depth of every pre-call brief Claude produces. Rather than generating a generic summary, it forces Claude to retrieve specific data points relevant to a cash investment conversation: investment policy indicators, banking relationships, credit profile signals, and prior contact history from your Drive files.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When preparing a pre-call brief for any contact, follow this exact structure and research sequence. Do not omit sections; mark any section "Not found" if data is unavailable rather than skipping it. Section 1 — Contact Profile: Full name, title, firm, tenure in current role (estimate from LinkedIn if not confirmed), prior roles relevant to cash or treasury, any published commentary or conference participation in the last 24 months. Section 2 — Firm Cash Profile: Reported cash and short-term investments from most recent 10-Q or 10-K (cite the filing date); year-over-year change in cash position; any public commentary on investment policy or liquidity strategy from earnings transcripts or investor day materials. Section 3 — Credit and Counterparty Signals: Current issuer credit rating from Moody's and S&P if available; any ratings actions or outlook changes in the last 12 months; recent M&A activity or capital structure changes that may affect cash levels. Section 4 — Banking Relationships: Known primary banking relationships from public filings or press releases; any named custodian, money market fund provider, or sweep account arrangement in public documents. Section 5 — Relationship History: Search my Google Drive Prospecting folder for any prior notes, briefs, or correspondence referencing this contact or their firm. Summarize what exists and flag the file name. Section 6 — Talking Points: Three specific, non-generic opening angles for this conversation, each grounded in a data point from sections above. Do not use generic value propositions. Each talking point should reference something specific about their firm's cash profile, credit situation, or recent news. Format the brief as a single Google Doc saved to my Prospecting/Call Briefs folder, named: [Contact Last Name]_[Firm]_[YYYY-MM-DD].doc

Skill 03

Outreach Voice and Compliance Guard

TriggerEmail drafting, outreach, follow-up
Connectors UsedApollo, Gmail, Google Sheets
OutputDraft email queue, flagged items

This skill governs tone, structure, and compliance-relevant constraints for all outreach drafts. It also instructs Claude to flag any draft where the proposed hook could be construed as an investment recommendation, a performance claim, or a statement requiring regulatory review, a meaningful safeguard when outreach references yield data, fund performance, or credit spreads.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When drafting any outreach email or follow-up message, apply the following rules without exception. Tone and register: Formal and direct. Do not use pleasantries such as "I hope this finds you well," "reaching out to connect," or "touch base." Do not use first-person superlatives or performance language. Do not reference AI assistance or automated research. Structure: Four to five sentences maximum. Sentence 1: a specific, researched observation about the recipient's firm (cash position, recent credit event, filing detail, conference commentary — sourced from data, not generic). Sentence 2–3: a relevant, non-promotional statement about how our cash investment capabilities are relevant to their situation. Sentence 4: a single, low-friction call to action (a 20-minute call, not a meeting or a pitch). No closing sentence beyond the call to action. Compliance flags — automatically add a [COMPLIANCE REVIEW] tag to any draft that: references a specific yield, rate, or return figure; names a specific fund or product by name; includes a forward-looking statement about market conditions; makes a comparative claim about performance relative to a benchmark or competitor. Do not remove these drafts from the output queue; flag them and continue. Subject lines: Specific and non-promotional. Reference the firm name or a concrete detail. Do not use questions, exclamation points, or engagement-bait constructions. Output all drafts to a Google Sheet named "Outreach Queue — [Month] [Year]" in my Prospecting folder, with columns: Contact Name, Firm, Email, Subject Line, Draft Body, Compliance Flag (Y/N), Flag Reason.

Skill 04

Counterparty Credit Monitor

TriggerCredit monitoring, counterparty research, watchlist
Connectors UsedMoody's MCP, S&P, FactSet, Claude in Chrome
OutputDrive summary, Gmail alert on material changes

This skill instructs Claude to apply a consistent monitoring framework when scanning for credit and counterparty developments. It defines what constitutes a material change requiring immediate notification versus a routine update logged to Drive, preventing alert fatigue while ensuring nothing consequential is missed.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When monitoring or researching counterparty credit developments, apply the following materiality framework. Immediate notification required (send Gmail summary in addition to Drive log): any ratings downgrade or negative watch placement by Moody's or S&P on an active counterparty; any downgrade that crosses an investment-grade threshold (below Baa3/BBB-); any regulatory action, enforcement order, or consent agreement involving a counterparty's banking or broker-dealer entity; any announced merger, acquisition, or change-of-control transaction involving a counterparty; any earnings release disclosing a material liquidity shortfall, covenant breach, or going-concern qualification. Routine log only (Drive doc update, no email): affirmation of existing ratings with stable outlook; earnings releases with no material credit indicators; personnel changes at counterparty firms; general news coverage without credit implications. For every item logged, record: counterparty name, event type, date, source, materiality classification (immediate/routine), and a two-sentence summary of the credit implication. Save to Google Drive in my Prospecting/Counterparty Monitor folder in a file named "Credit Monitor Log — [YYYY]." Append new entries; do not overwrite.

Skill 05

Conference Intelligence Extractor

TriggerConference research, event speakers, AFP, TMA, EuroFinance
Connectors UsedClaude in Chrome
OutputAnnotated contact additions to prospect sheet

Industry conferences (AFP National, TMA Annual, EuroFinance, Finadium, Strategic Treasurer Summit) are among the highest-signal sources for identifying active treasury decision-makers. This skill instructs Claude to extract structured contact intelligence from conference programs and speaker directories, cross-reference names against your existing prospect list, and add net-new names with context.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When researching a financial industry conference for prospecting intelligence, follow this extraction protocol. Target conferences: AFP National Conference, TMA Annual Conference, EuroFinance International Cash and Treasury Management, Finadium Investors in Securities Finance, Strategic Treasurer Summit, TEXPO, and any conference whose program includes a Treasury Management, Cash Investment, or Corporate Finance track. For each speaker or panelist found: extract full name, title, firm, session title, and session topic. Classify each by contact tier using the Treasury Contact Classifier skill (Tier 1, 2, or 3). Then cross-reference against my existing Google Sheet in Prospecting/Coverage Lists — if the name already exists, add the conference appearance to their record in the "Conference Signal" column. If the name is new and qualifies as Tier 1 or Tier 2, add them as a new row with source noted as the conference name and year. Do not add Tier 3 contacts from conferences unless they are speaking on a cash investment, short-duration, or liquidity management topic, in which case note the session title as the relevance hook. Flag any speaker at a competitor firm (ask me to confirm the competitor list if not already in project context) so I can review before outreach.

Skill 06

Market Rate and Instrument Digest

TriggerMarket digest, rate research, money market, CP, CD
Connectors UsedFactSet, LSEG, Claude in Chrome
OutputStructured digest, Drive doc

This skill standardizes the format and data sources for any market intelligence output, ensuring that rate and spread figures Claude pulls are cited with source and date, and that the digest structure is consistent enough to be scanned in under five minutes every morning.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When compiling any market intelligence digest or rate summary for a corporate cash investment context, apply the following structure and sourcing standards. Required data points (retrieve from FactSet or LSEG; note source and retrieval date next to each figure): — Overnight SOFR and EFFR (Federal Funds Effective Rate), prior day close — 1-month, 3-month, and 6-month Treasury bill yields, prior day close — 3-month SOFR term rate — A1/P1 commercial paper spread to T-bills, 30-day and 90-day tenors — Representative CD rates at systemically important banks, 3-month tenor — 7-day and 30-day prime money market fund yields (use Crane Data or iMoneyNet if accessible via browser; otherwise note "MMF yield data unavailable — check Crane Data manually") Format: Present as a structured table with instrument, rate/yield, prior day change (bps), and source. Follow the table with a two-paragraph narrative: the first paragraph states what moved and by how much; the second flags any spread widening, curve inversion, or policy-related development worth noting for a short-duration portfolio. Do not editorialize or forecast. Cite only what the data shows. Close with a one-paragraph summary of any credit or issuer news relevant to the CP/CD market from overnight wire services.

Skill 07

Relationship Status Tracker

TriggerCRM update, relationship log, follow-up, pipeline
Connectors UsedGmail, Google Drive, Apollo
OutputUpdated pipeline sheet, follow-up queue

This skill governs how Claude maintains the relationship pipeline log: what counts as a touchpoint, how status fields are updated, and when a contact should be surfaced for follow-up. It replaces the manual CRM update step that most relationship managers skip under time pressure, ensuring the pipeline remains accurate without requiring deliberate maintenance.

→ Skill Creation Prompt — Paste into Skill Body

When updating or auditing the relationship pipeline, apply the following status taxonomy and update rules. Pipeline status definitions: — Identified: contact found, not yet reached — Contacted: first outreach sent, no reply — Engaged: reply received or meeting confirmed — Active: meeting held, follow-up in progress — Warm: ongoing dialogue, no immediate action item — Dormant: no response after two follow-up attempts; last contact over 90 days ago — Closed/Not Pursuing: contact declined engagement or no longer meets criteria Update rules: When scanning Gmail for sent messages and replies, update status automatically for any contact whose email address appears in my Prospecting coverage sheet. If a reply is received from a contact at Engaged or lower, escalate to Engaged and note the date. If a contact has been in "Contacted" status for more than 14 days with no reply, move to a follow-up queue. If a contact has been Dormant for more than 180 days, flag for manual review rather than removing. Follow-up queue output: A tab in the Prospecting pipeline sheet named "Follow-Up Required" listing all contacts where a follow-up action is due, sorted by days since last touch (descending). Include: name, firm, tier, current status, last touch date, and a suggested follow-up action (re-engagement email, LinkedIn check, phone). Run this update automatically whenever I ask for a pipeline review, a follow-up audit, or a coverage status report.

Appendix D

Claude Cowork + GitHub

How to connect Cowork to your GitHub repository so Claude can publish polished HTML analysis pages as live websites, maintain a version-controlled backup of your prospect data, and do all of it autonomously, without you touching a terminal.

GitHub is not just a place to store code. For a cash investment SVP running a Cowork-assisted practice, it serves two distinct functions. First, it is a publishing platform: any HTML file pushed to a GitHub Pages-enabled repository becomes a live, publicly accessible URL within 30 seconds. Second, it is an automatic, version-controlled backup: every file Claude commits carries a timestamp, a change description, and a full history of every prior version. Neither function requires you to understand git or write a single line of code. Claude handles every interaction with GitHub on your behalf.

This appendix covers the setup once, explains the two use cases in depth, and provides the exact Cowork prompts to operate each workflow from that point forward.

Why GitHub for a Finance Prospecting Practice

Most practitioners in your role manage analysis outputs and contact data through a combination of email attachments, shared Drive folders, and CRM exports. The problem with that architecture is distribution friction. Sending a prospect a PDF of your weekly market analysis means they receive a file, file it somewhere, and read it once if at all. Sending them a URL to a clean, well-designed webpage that updates every week, carries your name in the domain, and renders perfectly on any device — is a qualitatively different thing. It positions you as a firm that produces institutional-quality research rather than someone who sends attachments.

GitHub Pages makes this possible at no cost. Every repository on GitHub can have an associated Pages site at a URL in the format username.github.io/repository-name. Claude can publish to that URL automatically as part of any routine that produces an HTML output. The prospect receives a link, not a file. The link is always current.

The backup use case is equally straightforward. GitHub maintains a full version history of every file it tracks. If Claude commits your master coverage sheet as a CSV every Friday, you have 52 timestamped snapshots at the end of the year, each recoverable in one command. If a routine accidentally overwrites a field, the prior version is one step away. If you want to see what your pipeline looked like three months ago, that record exists without you having taken any deliberate action to preserve it.

Part 1 — Setup: Connecting Cowork to GitHub

The connection requires three things: a GitHub account, a Personal Access Token (PAT) that gives Claude permission to push files, and a one-time project configuration inside Cowork. Once configured, every subsequent GitHub interaction is handled by Claude with no further credential management required on your part.

Step 01

Create Your GitHub Repository

Go to github.com and sign in. Click the + icon in the top right and select New repository. Name it something like cash-investment-research. Set it to Public (required for free GitHub Pages hosting) or Private if your firm's compliance policy requires it; note that private repos require a paid GitHub plan for Pages. Leave all other settings at their defaults and click Create repository.

You now have an empty repository. You will not need to interact with it manually again after this setup — Claude will handle all future file operations.

Step 02

Generate a Personal Access Token

The PAT is a credential that allows Claude to push files to your repository on your behalf. It is the only credential you will ever need to handle manually for this workflow.

In GitHub, go to Settings (your profile, top right) → Developer settings → Personal access tokens → Tokens (classic) → Generate new token (classic). Give it a name like "Claude Cowork Integration." Set expiration to No expiration or a date far in the future. Under Select scopes, check only repo (this grants full repository read/write). Click Generate token and copy the value immediately — it is shown only once.

Security Note

Treat this token like a password. Do not paste it into emails, shared documents, or chat interfaces other than directly into your Cowork project context as described in Step 3. If it is ever compromised, revoke it immediately from the same GitHub settings page and generate a new one.

Step 03

Add GitHub Credentials to Your Cowork Project

Open Claude Desktop, go to your Finance Prospecting project, and open Project Settings → Context. Add the following lines to the bottom of your existing project context instruction set, substituting your actual values:

→ Add to Project Context

GitHub integration is enabled for this project. GitHub username: [your GitHub username] Repository name: [your repository name, e.g. cash-investment-research] Personal Access Token: [paste your PAT here] Default commit author: [Your Name] <[your email]> Pages URL: https://[username].github.io/[repository-name]/ When pushing files to GitHub, always commit with a descriptive message that includes the date and the nature of the content. Never push files containing raw personal data (phone numbers, email addresses) to a public repository — export those fields as blank or hashed in any file destined for GitHub.

Claude will reference these values whenever a task involves GitHub. The PAT is stored only in your project context, which is private to your Cowork session and never shared or used outside it.

Step 04

Enable GitHub Pages on the Repository

This is a one-time step Claude can execute for you. Paste this task into Cowork after completing Steps 1–3:

Create a file at .github/workflows/deploy.yml in my repository with a GitHub Actions workflow that deploys the repository contents to GitHub Pages on every push to the main branch. Use actions/checkout@v4, actions/configure-pages@v5, actions/upload-pages-artifact@v3, and actions/deploy-pages@v4. Set permissions for pages write and id-token write. 2. Part 2 — Use Case One: Publishing Weekly Analysis as a Live Website

The core idea is this: instead of sending a prospect a PDF or an email with inline market commentary, you send them a URL. That URL hosts a clean, well-designed webpage with your analysis, your name, and your firm's positioning — formatted to the same standard as this guide. It is always current, always accessible, and carries a level of production quality that a PDF attachment does not.

The workflow requires no manual steps after setup. Claude generates the HTML, pushes it to GitHub, and the page goes live. The prospect receives a link in your outreach email. When you update the analysis next week, the same URL reflects the new content automatically — or you publish a new dated URL and send an update. Both approaches have merit depending on your relationship with the recipient.

What a Prospect-Facing Analysis Page Should Contain

The most effective format for a cash investment audience is a one-page market brief with three sections: the current rate environment (SOFR, T-bill yields, CP spreads, MMF yields — the same data from the Morning Market Open routine), a short-duration portfolio perspective (what the rate environment implies for cash investors with a 0–12 month horizon), and a section specific to the prospect's situation (their firm's cash balance, any relevant credit or earnings development, a tailored observation connecting market conditions to their specific position). The third section is what makes it worth sending. Generic market commentary is available everywhere. A page that references their firm's Q1 earnings and connects that to a specific rate move is not.

On Public vs Private Pages

Analysis pages published to GitHub Pages are publicly accessible — anyone with the URL can read them, but the URL is not indexed or discoverable unless you share it. This is effectively security-by-obscurity, which is appropriate for market commentary but not for anything containing client-specific data, portfolio positions, or non-public information. The page should contain only market observations and publicly available firm data. Anything proprietary stays in Drive.

Part 3 — Use Case Two: GitHub as Prospect Data Backup

Your master coverage sheet, outreach queue, and credit monitor log represent months of accumulated research. They live in Google Drive, which is reliable, but Drive provides no version history on Sheets beyond its own 30-day revision history, no ability to restore a specific named snapshot from six months ago, and no structured audit trail that shows what changed, when, and why. GitHub provides all three as a standard feature of how it works.

The mechanism is simple: at the end of each week, Claude exports your key data files from Google Drive as CSV files and commits them to a private subdirectory in your GitHub repository. Each commit is timestamped and carries a message describing the state of the pipeline that week. Over time, this creates a complete, searchable history of your coverage practice — every contact added, every status change, every outreach campaign — recoverable to any point in time.

The backup repository should be private. Contact names, email addresses, and relationship notes are personal data; they should not be publicly accessible on a GitHub Pages site. This is a separate repository from the one used for publishing analysis pages, or a private subdirectory that is excluded from the Pages deployment via a .gitignore configuration — Claude will set this up correctly when you run the initialization routine below.

How This Changes Your Prospect Outreach

The practical effect of combining Cowork, weekly analysis pages, and GitHub Pages is that your outreach emails shift from generic introductions to delivery vehicles for specific, timely research. An email that says "I wanted to introduce myself and discuss your cash investment needs" competes with every other coverage email in your prospect's inbox. An email that says "I published this week's rate brief with a note on [their firm]'s CP issuance position in the current spread environment — link below" does not have many competitors.

The setup cost is a one-time 20-minute configuration. The ongoing cost is the time Claude spends generating and publishing the analysis — roughly 15–20 minutes of automated work every Friday afternoon, producing an asset that serves every active coverage relationship simultaneously. Each prospect who receives a link to their own firm-specific page gets something that took Claude 20 minutes to produce but reads as if you spent an afternoon on it.

The Index Page

Once you have published several weekly briefs, consider asking Claude to generate an index page — a clean landing page at the root of your GitHub Pages site that lists all published briefs in reverse chronological order, with a one-line headline for each. This gives prospects who revisit the URL a library of your prior analysis rather than a single dated page. Claude can regenerate this index automatically as part of the weekly publish routine: "After pushing the weekly brief, regenerate index.html at the repository root to include this week's brief in the list of published analysis pages."

On Compliance Review Before Publishing

Any analysis page that will be shared with prospects should go through your firm's standard marketing and communications review process before the URL is distributed — the same process that governs any client-facing written communication. The fact that it is a webpage rather than a document does not change its regulatory character. Claude can flag each HTML page for review before pushing by adding a step: "Before committing this file to GitHub, save a copy to Prospecting/Compliance Review/[filename] in Google Drive and send me a Gmail asking me to confirm compliance clearance before the file goes live." Claude will then wait for your confirmation before executing the push.
